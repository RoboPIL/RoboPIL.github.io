BaB-ND: Long-Horizon Motion Planning with Branch-and-Bound and Neural Dynamics

1University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, 2Columbia University
* Equal Contribution
Paper arXiv Video Code


BaB-ND uses branch-and-bound method to plan long-horizon trajectories using neural dynamics model.

Main Contributions

  • General BaB-Based Framework: We propose a widely applicable branch-and-bound (BaB) framework for effective long-horizon motion planning over neural dynamics models.
  • Novel Procedures for Planning: Our framework introduces innovative branching, bounding, and searching methods, inspired by neural network verification algorithms, specifically adapted for planning tasks involving neural dynamics models.
  • Demonstrated Effectiveness and Scalability: We showcase the framework's effectiveness, applicability, and scalability across a range of complex planning problems, including contact-rich manipulation, handling deformable objects, and managing object piles, utilizing diverse model architectures.

Video

Method Overview


We propose a branch-and-bound framework for long-horizon planning, involving branching, bounding, and searching. The branching procedure systematically divides the search space with a heuristic. The bounding procedure efficiently evaluates the lower bounds of objectives. The searching procedure can find high-quality solutions to objectives in sub-domains.

Tasks


We evaluate our framework on four complex robotic manipulation tasks involving non-smooth objectives, non-convex feasible regions and requiring long action sequences. Different architectures of neural dynamics like MLP and GNN are leveraged for different scenarios.
